    Tutorial

    This tutorial aims to guide you through the process of creating slides that adhere to the principle of simplicity.

    Step 1: Define Your Core Message

    Before you delve into the mechanics, clarify your core message. This one-liner or single thought will be your guide as you make design decisions.

    Step 2: Open Your Presentation Software

    Open your preferred presentation software—be it Microsoft PowerPoint, Apple Keynote, or another platform—and start with a blank canvas.

    Step 3: Optimize Signal-to-Noise Ratio

    • Use Titles Wisely: Slide titles should encapsulate the slide's main point.
    • Avoid Bullet Points: Utilize short phrases or single words to enhance your verbal message.
    • Eliminate Noise: Remove anything that does not directly support your core message, improving the signal-to-noise ratio.

    Step 4: Leverage High-Quality Images

    • Select Images Carefully: Choose images that align with your core message.
    • High Resolution: Use sharp, high-quality images to avoid pixelation.
    • Full-Screen Impact: Utilize full-screen images as backgrounds to make a strong emotional connection.

    Step 5: Master Font and Color

    • Keep Fonts Consistent: Stick to two types of fonts for a clean look.
    • Color Harmony: Implement a simple, non-distracting color scheme, preferably limiting yourself to three colors plus black and white.

    Step 6: Utilize Empty Space and Repetition

    • Eliminate Clutter: Remove any elements that detract from your message, adhering to the 'less is more' philosophy.
    • Whitespace: Allow for empty space or "breathing room" around text and images. This enhances focus and readability.
    • Repetition: Use repetition in design elements to create visual harmony and easier navigation for your audience.

    Step 7: Review, Revise, and Seek Feedback

    • Read Aloud: Run through your slides, speaking aloud to ensure they complement your narration.
    • Peer Review: Seek a fresh pair of eyes to review your slides. They might catch distractions or noise that you missed.
