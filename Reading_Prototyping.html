    Narrative

    When we think of a narrative, we think of stories that are engaging, structured, and emotive. In a UI/UX career, understanding the power of narrative is critical. But why?

    Just like how humans have passed down information through stories for generations, we still do today using digital interfaces. A well-structured user experience should guide the user through a journey, just like a good story. It should engage the user emotionally and logically, making the interaction memorable and intuitive. This is why many successful apps and websites have a clear beginning (introduction to objective), a structured middle (interactions), and a definitive end (finished objective). If a UI/UX professional can harness the power of narrative, they can create more effective and engaging user experiences.

    Prototyping in Figma

    FreeForm

    I watched this video

    The video showed me a bunch of cool ways to use Figma for designing and making prototypes. It talked about making animations, working with shapes using something called Boolean groups, and how to manage text, colors, and files. What really caught my eye was seeing Figma used to make presentations or slides. I never thought of it as a tool for that, so it was a cool new way to think about what Figma can do. The video probably went on to show how all these features in Figma come together nicely, making it easier for designers like me to keep things organized and share our ideas in a fun and interactive way through presentations.
